<p><p>Phenolic resins can be classified into two types in the photoresist market: flake and block. Flake phenolic resins are in the form of irregular flakes, which provide good solubility and flow properties. They are commonly used in the production of photoresist formulations due to their excellent film-forming capabilities. On the other hand, block phenolic resins are formed as solid blocks and are known for their excellent chemical resistance and dimensional stability. They find applications in photoresist formulations where high thermal stability and resistance to solvents are required.</p></p>
