<p><p>Plasterboard liner, also known as drywall liner or gypsum liner, is a type of building material used as a protective layer on walls or ceilings before the final decoration is applied. It is made from a sheet of gypsum wrapped in a special liner paper, which provides strength, durability, and fire-resistant properties to the walls or ceilings.</p><p>The current outlook of the plasterboard liner market is positive, and it is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The market is primarily driven by the growing demand for housing and infrastructure development in both residential and commercial sectors. Additionally, the increasing awareness regarding the benefits of using plasterboard liners, such as fire resistance, thermal insulation, and soundproofing, is fueling the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the emergence of eco-friendly and recyclable plasterboard liners is also boosting the market. These liners are made from recycled materials and have low carbon footprint, making them attractive to environmentally conscious consumers. The market is also witnessing innovations such as moisture-resistant plasterboard liners, which are gaining popularity in areas with high humidity or prone to water damage.</p><p>However, certain factors may challenge the market growth, such as fluctuations in raw material prices. The cost of gypsum, a key material used in plasterboard liners, can vary depending on supply and demand dynamics. Additionally, stringent regulations regarding environmental impact and product standards may also hamper market growth.</p><p>Despite these challenges, the plasterboard liner market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.10% during the forecasted period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing construction activities in emerging economies, rising urbanization, and the growing preference for sustainable building materials. Overall, the future of the plasterboard liner market looks promising, driven by various factors that support its demand and adoption in the construction industry.</p></p>

<p><p>The plasterboard liner market consists of two main types: plasterboard liner face and plasterboard liner back. Plasterboard liner face refers to the front-facing side of the plasterboard, which is typically smooth and used as the visible surface for wall or ceiling installations. On the other hand, plasterboard liner back is the backside of the plasterboard, which is often rough or textured and provides structural support. Both types play essential roles in construction projects, with the liner face serving an aesthetic purpose and the liner back offering strength and stability.</p></p>
