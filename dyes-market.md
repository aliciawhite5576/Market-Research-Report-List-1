<p><p>Dyes are widely used to color textiles, and the market can be categorized into different types based on the chemical composition and application. Disperse dyes are suitable for synthetic fibers like polyester, while reactive dyes effectively color natural fibers, such as cotton, through a chemical reaction. Sulfur dyes are mainly used for cellulosic fibers and offer excellent color fastness. Vat dyes are known for their high color intensity and are commonly used for cotton fabrics. Acid dyes are suitable for protein fibers like silk and wool. Other market segments may include Direct, Basic, and Azoic dyes, each with its unique application areas and properties.</p></p>
