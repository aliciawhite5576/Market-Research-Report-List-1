<p><p>Fire-proof sealants are used to prevent the spread of fire by forming a protective barrier. Elastomeric type sealants are flexible and stretchable, making them suitable for areas that experience movement or vibration. They are typically used in construction joints and gaps. Intumescent type sealants expand when exposed to heat, forming an insulating char layer that helps to slow down the progress of fire. They are commonly used in fire-rated walls, doors, and windows. Both types of sealants are essential for enhancing fire safety in various structures.</p></p>
