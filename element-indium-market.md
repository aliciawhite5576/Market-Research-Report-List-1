<p><p>Indium is a chemical element with the symbol In and atomic number 49. It is a soft and malleable metal that is commonly used in various applications due to its unique properties. Indium is highly resistant to corrosion, making it suitable for use in alloys, solders, and as a coating on important components. Additionally, it has a low melting point and is transparent to visible light, enabling its use in thin-film technologies like solar panels and liquid crystal displays (LCDs).</p><p>The global indium market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.7% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for LCDs in various industries, especially electronics and automotive, is driving the demand for indium. LCD screens are widely used in smartphones, televisions, and other electronic devices, thereby boosting the demand for indium in the market.</p><p>Secondly, the rising popularity of renewable energy sources is also contributing to the growth of the indium market. Indium is used in the production of solar panels, which are experiencing high demand due to the increased focus on clean and sustainable energy solutions.</p><p>Furthermore, the expanding applications of indium in the aerospace and defense industries are also fueling its market growth. Indium is used in the manufacturing of bearings, seals, and O-rings, which are critical components in these industries.</p><p>However, the indium market faces a challenge regarding its limited availability. Indium is primarily obtained as a by-product of zinc mining, and its scarcity and increasing demand may lead to supply chain disruptions. As a result, research and development efforts are focused on finding alternative materials or improving recycling techniques to ensure a sustainable supply of indium in the future.</p><p>Overall, the indium market is poised for steady growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing demand for LCDs, solar panels, and its applications in various industries.</p></p>
