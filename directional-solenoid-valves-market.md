<p><p>Directional solenoid valves are categorized into two types: Type I and Type II. Type I valves are designed with a single coil, which creates a magnet when energized, causing the valve to shift and change the direction of flow. On the other hand, Type II valves consist of dual coils, allowing the valve to shift and change the direction of flow independently. Both types are widely used in various industries where precise control of fluid flow is required, such as pneumatic systems, hydraulic systems, and automotive applications.</p></p>
