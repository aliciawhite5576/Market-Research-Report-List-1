<p><ul><li>Low-Carbon Steel</li><li>High-Carbon Steel</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The carbon steel blades market can be categorized into two types: low-carbon steel and high-carbon steel. Low-carbon steel blades have a lower carbon content, usually below 0.3%, which makes them softer and more flexible. They are commonly used in applications that require sharpness but can tolerate some bending, such as utility knives. On the other hand, high-carbon steel blades have a higher carbon content, typically ranging from 0.6% to 1%, making them harder and stronger. They are preferred for applications that demand durability and resistance to wear, like chef knives.</p></p>
