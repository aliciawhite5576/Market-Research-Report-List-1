<p><p>Advanced carbon materials can be categorized into various types. Carbon fibers are high-strength, lightweight materials used in aerospace, automotive, and sports industries. Graphenes are single-layered carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice, possessing exceptional electrical and thermal conductivity. Carbon nanotubes exhibit extraordinary strength and electrical properties, finding applications in electronics and energy storage. Structural graphites provide high thermal stability and are used in high-temperature applications. Carbon foams, known for their low density and high porosity, are employed in aerospace and filtration systems. Lastly, the "Others" category encompasses various carbon materials with unique properties suited for specific applications.</p></p>
