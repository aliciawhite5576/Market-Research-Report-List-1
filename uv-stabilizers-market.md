<p><p>UV stabilizers are additives used to protect materials and products from the harmful effects of ultraviolet (UV) light. There are three main types of UV stabilizers in the market. Hindered Amine Light Stabilizers (HALS) work by trapping free radicals generated by UV radiation, thus preventing material degradation. UV absorbers absorb UV radiation, converting it into harmless heat. Quenchers work by deactivating free radicals. Each type offers different benefits and is used in various applications, such as plastics, coatings, and textiles, to enhance their durability and lifespan by preventing UV-induced damage.</p></p>
