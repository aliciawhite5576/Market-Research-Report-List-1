<p><p>Headlamps for passengers come in different market types. Halogen lamps are a common type that produce light by passing electricity through a filament, but they are not very efficient. Xenon lights, also known as high-intensity discharge (HID) lamps, use xenon gas to create a brighter, blue-white light. LED headlamps are energy-efficient and produce a bright, pure light. Other market types may include adaptive headlights that adjust according to driving conditions, laser headlights that provide enhanced visibility, and matrix LED headlights that can selectively adjust the beam pattern.</p></p>
