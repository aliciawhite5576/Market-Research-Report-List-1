<p><ul><li>High Carbon Content</li><li>Low Carbon Content</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Original charcoal is classified into two market types based on carbon content: high carbon content and low carbon content. High carbon content charcoal is produced by burning wood in a limited oxygen environment, resulting in a high percentage of carbon content. It burns at high temperatures and is preferred for industrial use, such as metallurgy and steelmaking. In contrast, low carbon content charcoal is produced by burning wood in an open-air environment, resulting in a lower percentage of carbon content. It burns at a lower temperature and is commonly used for cooking and heating purposes.</p></p>
