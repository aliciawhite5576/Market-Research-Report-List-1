<p><p>Immunohistochemistry (IHC) testing is a technique used to identify specific proteins or antigens in tissues through the use of antibodies. The Traditional Direct Method involves directly conjugating the antibody to a visible label. The New Direct Method uses non-conjugated antibodies and a secondary antibody with a visible label. The Enzyme Bridge method incorporates an intermediate enzyme-conjugated antibody. The Peroxidase-Antiperoxidase (PAP) Method uses a secondary antibody and peroxidase-antiperoxidase complex for visualization. These different methods offer various advantages in terms of sensitivity, specificity, and signal/background ratio.</p></p>
