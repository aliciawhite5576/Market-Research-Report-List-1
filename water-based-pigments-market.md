<p><p>The water-based pigment market is divided into two categories: organic water-based pigments and inorganic water-based pigments. Organic water-based pigments are derived from carbon-based compounds and are highly versatile, offering a wide range of vibrant colors. They are commonly used in industries like textiles, cosmetics, and printing inks. In contrast, inorganic water-based pigments are made from mineral compounds and offer excellent lightfastness and stability. They are often used in applications such as paints, coatings, and plastics, where durability is crucial. Both types of pigments provide unique advantages and cater to different market demands.</p></p>
