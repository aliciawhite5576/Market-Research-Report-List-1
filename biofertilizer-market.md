<p><p>Biofertilizers are a type of organic fertilizers that enhance soil fertility and plant growth. The market for biofertilizers can be categorized into four types. Nitrogen-fixing biofertilizers contain nitrogen-fixing bacteria that convert atmospheric nitrogen into a plant-usable form, promoting plant growth. Phosphate-solubilizing biofertilizers contain microorganisms that solubilize insoluble phosphates into plant-available forms, enhancing root development. Potash-mobilizing biofertilizers consist of microorganisms that release potassium from mineral reserves, supporting plant metabolism. Other biofertilizers may include those that fix atmospheric carbon, improve soil structure, or provide micronutrients to plants, boosting overall crop yield.</p></p>
