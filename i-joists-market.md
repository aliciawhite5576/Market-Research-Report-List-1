<p><p>I-joists, also known as engineered wood I-joists, are a type of manufactured wood product commonly used in the construction industry. They are designed to provide superior strength and stability for floor and roof framing systems.</p><p>I-joists consist of two flanges made of structural composite lumber (SCL) or laminated veneer lumber (LVL) that are connected by a web made of oriented strand board (OSB) or plywood. This construction method allows I-joists to have a higher strength-to-weight ratio compared to traditional solid wood joists.</p><p>In terms of market analysis, the global I-joists market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for cost-effective and environmentally-friendly building materials, as well as the growing construction industry, are the key factors driving market growth.</p><p>Additionally, I-joists offer advantages such as longer spans, dimensional stability, and ease of installation, which further propel their demand in the market. The rising adoption of building codes and regulations focusing on energy efficiency is also expected to drive the demand for I-joists.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing several trends. OSB I-joists are manufactured using wood strands compressed and bonded together, making them cost-effective and widely used.
