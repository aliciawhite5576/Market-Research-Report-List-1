<p><p>Petroleum based hydrocarbon resin market is categorized into different types based on their composition and properties. C5 resins are derived from the cracking of petroleum naphtha and mainly used in adhesives, rubber, and sealants. C9 resins are obtained from thermal cracking of crude oil and are commonly used in paints, printing inks, and rubber compounding. Hydrogenated hydrocarbon resins are produced by adding hydrogen to C5 and C9 resins, resulting in improved stability and increased compatibility with other materials. C5/C9 resins are a blend of C5 and C9 resins, offering a balanced combination of properties for various applications.</p></p>
