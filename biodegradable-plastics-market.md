<p><p>Biodegradable plastics are a type of plastic material that can break down naturally over time, reducing the environmental impact. There are various types of biodegradable plastics in the market. PLA (Polylactic Acid) is made from renewable sources like cornstarch. Starch blends combine starch and other materials for increased flexibility. PCL (Polycaprolactone) is a versatile polymer. Regenerated cellulose is derived from wood pulp and is often used in film applications. PBS (Polybutylene Succinate) offers excellent mechanical properties. PHA (Polyhydroxyalkanoates) is produced through microbial fermentation and is highly versatile. These different types cater to various applications and have unique properties.</p></p>
