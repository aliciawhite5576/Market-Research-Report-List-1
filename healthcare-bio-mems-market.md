Microfluidic chips are small devices that manipulate and control fluids at a microscopic scale for medical diagnostics and drug delivery. Pressure sensors measure and monitor blood pressure, intracranial pressure, and other bodily pressures. Accelerometers measure acceleration and movement, helping in applications like patient activity monitoring and fall detection. Microphones capture sound for hearing aids and voice recognition applications. Flow meters measure the flow rate of fluids such as blood or medication. Other Bio-MEMS devices exist for various applications like glucose monitoring, oxygen sensing, and tissue engineering.</p></p>
