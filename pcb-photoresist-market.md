<p><p>PCB photoresist is a type of material used in the manufacturing of printed circuit boards (PCBs). There are two main types of PCB photoresist: positive photoresist and negative photoresist. </p><p>Positive photoresist is applied to a substrate, and when exposed to UV light, it becomes soluble in the developer solution. The exposed areas are therefore removed during development, leaving behind the desired pattern.</p><p>In contrast, negative photoresist becomes insoluble upon exposure to UV light. The areas that are exposed harden and protect the underlying substrate during development, while the unexposed portions are removed.</p><p>Both types play a critical role in the PCB fabrication process by enabling precise patterning and etching of the circuitry.</p></p>
