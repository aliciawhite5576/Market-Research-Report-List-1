<p><p>Non-regulating choke valves are used to control the flow of fluids in various industries. They can be classified into three market types based on their operating mechanism - pneumatic, hydraulic, and electric.</p><p>1. Pneumatic choke valves are powered by compressed air and are primarily used in applications where precise control over fluid flow is required. They offer fast response times and are suitable for environments with high vibration or explosive atmospheres.</p><p>2. Hydraulic choke valves use hydraulic fluid to control the flow rate. They are known for their reliability and high force output, making them ideal for applications that require heavy-duty performance and operate under extreme pressures or temperatures.</p><p>3. Electric choke valves are motor-driven and offer precise control over flow regulation. They are commonly used in automation systems and industries that require remote operation or integration with other electronic devices. Electric choke valves provide efficient and accurate control of fluid flow rates.</p></p>
