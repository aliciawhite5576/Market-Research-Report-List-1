<p><p>Low Density Polyethylene (LDPE) is a type of plastic with a lower density compared to other forms of polyethylene. It is commonly used in various applications due to its flexibility, transparency, and moisture resistance. LDPE can be categorized into different market types, namely C4, C6, and C8. These market types are based on the comonomer used during the production of LDPE. C4 LDPE is made with butene-1, C6 LDPE is made with hexene-1, and C8 LDPE is made with octene-1. The choice of comonomer affects the physical properties and performance of LDPE in different applications.</p></p>
