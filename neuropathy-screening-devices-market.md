<p><p>Neuropathy screening devices can be classified into two main types: non-electronic devices and electronic devices. Non-electronic devices include tools such as tuning forks, monofilaments, and pinprick testers. These devices assess the patient's sensitivity to stimuli and help identify potential nerve damage. On the other hand, electronic devices, like nerve conduction studies (NCS) and electromyography (EMG) machines, utilize electrical impulses to measure nerve signals and evaluate their functioning. These devices provide more advanced and precise measurements, aiding in the diagnosis and monitoring of neuropathy conditions.</p></p>
