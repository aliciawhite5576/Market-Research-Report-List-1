<p><p>Asthma and COPD devices include inhalers and nebulizers. Inhalers are handheld devices that deliver medication directly into the lungs, providing quick relief for asthma and COPD symptoms. They come in different types, such as metered-dose inhalers (MDIs) and dry powder inhalers (DPIs), with each delivering the medication in a different way. Nebulizers, on the other hand, are larger machines that convert liquid medication into a fine mist, which is inhaled through a mask or mouthpiece. Nebulizers are often used for individuals who have difficulty using inhalers effectively. Both devices play an essential role in managing respiratory diseases like asthma and COPD.</p></p>
