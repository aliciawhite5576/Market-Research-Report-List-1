<p><p>The soil conditioner market is divided into two types: natural and synthetic. Natural soil conditioners are derived from organic materials, such as compost, animal manure, and peat moss, which improve soil quality by enhancing its structure, water retention, and nutrient content. Synthetic soil conditioners, on the other hand, are derived from chemical compounds and are manufactured to provide specific benefits to the soil, such as increasing its pH or reducing soil compaction. Both types of soil conditioners serve the purpose of improving soil health and fertility, but they differ in their sources and production methods.</p></p>
