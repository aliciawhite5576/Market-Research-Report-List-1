<p><p>Citrus concentrates are highly concentrated forms of citrus fruit juices, obtained by removing most of the water content from the juice. The process of making citrus concentrates involves extracting the juice from citrus fruits, filtering it, and then evaporating the water content to obtain a concentrated form of the juice. This enables easy storage, transportation, and longer shelf life without losing the essential properties and flavor of the juice.</p><p>The global citrus concentrates market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years, primarily due to the increasing demand for natural and healthy fruit-based products. The growing awareness about the health benefits associated with citrus fruits, such as high vitamin C content and antioxidant properties, has boosted the demand for citrus concentrates. Additionally, the convenience and versatility of citrus concentrates in various applications like beverages, dairy products, bakery, confectionery, and sauces have further contributed to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising consumer preference for clean-label products and the demand for natural flavors and additives have also driven the market for citrus concentrates. Manufacturers are focusing on developing new and innovative flavors to cater to consumers' changing taste preferences. They are also introducing ready-to-drink citrus-based beverages and functional beverages with added nutritional benefits to capture a larger consumer base.</p><p>Geographically, the market is dominated by North America and Europe, owing to the high consumption of citrus-based beverages and processed food products in these regions.
