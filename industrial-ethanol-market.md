<p><p>Industrial ethanol is classified into two main market types: agriculture ethanol and synthetic ethanol. Agricultural ethanol is produced from crops such as corn, sugarcane, or wheat through the process of fermentation. It is predominantly used as a biofuel additive in gasoline or as feedstock for chemicals synthesis. Synthetic ethanol, on the other hand, is produced from non-renewable sources like natural gas or coal through a chemical reaction process. It is primarily used as an industrial solvent or as a raw material for the production of plastics, detergents, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
