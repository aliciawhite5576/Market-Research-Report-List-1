<p><p>Flight recorders, also known as black boxes, are crucial devices installed in airplanes to capture valuable data during flights and assist in accident investigations. There are two main types of flight recorders: the Flight Data Recorder (FDR) and the Cockpit Voice Recorder (CVR). The FDR records various parameters such as altitude, speed, and control inputs, providing insights into the aircraft's performance. On the other hand, the CVR captures all audio communications within the cockpit, including conversations between pilots and air traffic controllers. Together, these recorders aid in comprehensively understanding incidents or accidents in the aviation industry.</p></p>
