<p><p>Dental resin 3D printers are categorized into various types based on their technology. Vat photopolymerization printers use a liquid resin that hardens layer by layer using UV light. Fused deposition modeling printers heat and extrude a thermoplastic material to form the desired objects. PolyJet technology printers jet and cure liquid photopolymer resins using UV light. Selective laser sintering printers use lasers to fuse powdered resin layers. Other types include digital light processing and stereolithography. These technologies enable dental professionals to create accurate and customized dental models, prosthetics, and orthodontic devices.</p></p>
