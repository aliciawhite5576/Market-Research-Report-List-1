<p><p>Diamond is a precious gemstone that is known for its luster, durability, and exceptional hardness. It is widely used in various industries, including jewelry, technology, and manufacturing. There are three main categories of the diamond market: rough, polished, and jewelry.</p><p>Rough diamonds are the raw form of diamonds that are extracted from mines. They are uncut and unpolished, and their value is determined by factors such as carat weight, color, clarity, and shape. Rough diamonds are primarily used by diamond manufacturers and wholesalers, who cut and polish them to create polished diamonds.</p><p>Polished diamonds are the result of cutting, polishing, and shaping the rough diamonds. They are graded based on the 4Cs (carat weight, color, clarity, and cut) and are used in various applications, including jewelry, industrial tools, and electronics.</p><p>The diamond jewelry market is the largest segment in the diamond industry, accounting for a significant share of the overall market. Diamond jewelry is highly sought after for its beauty, rarity, and value. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for diamond jewelry, especially in emerging economies, is driving the market growth. Rising disposable incomes and changing consumer preferences towards luxury goods are contributing to this demand.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements in diamond cutting and polishing techniques have led to improved quality and higher yields, making diamonds more affordable and accessible to a wider range of consumers.</p><p>Lastly, the rising popularity of lab-grown diamonds, which are chemically and physically identical to natural diamonds, is also impacting the diamond market. Lab-grown diamonds offer a more sustainable and ethical alternative to mined diamonds, attracting environmentally conscious consumers.</p><p>Overall, the diamond (rough, polished & jewelry) market is experiencing steady growth, driven by increasing demand, technological advancements, and the emergence of lab-grown diamonds.</p></p>
