<p><p>Hollow brick, also known as hollow concrete block or hollow block, is a building material mainly used in construction for its strength, durability, and thermal insulation properties. Several factors contribute to this positive outlook. Hollow bricks also contribute to sustainable construction practices by reducing the use of natural resources, such as clay, and minimizing energy consumption during production.</p><p>Furthermore, growing urbanization and industrialization in emerging economies, coupled with government initiatives for infrastructure development, are likely to support the growth of the hollow brick market. <p><p>The hollow brick market is divided into several types. Stretcher blocks are the most commonly used hollow bricks that are laid in a straight line, purely for the purpose of stretching the wall. Corner blocks have a perpendicular groove on one end, allowing the bricks to be placed at corners. Pillar blocks, as the name suggests, are used for constructing vertical pillars or columns. The "others" category includes various types of hollow bricks that serve specific purposes, such as jamb blocks for door and window frames or lintel blocks for supporting load above openings.</p></p>
