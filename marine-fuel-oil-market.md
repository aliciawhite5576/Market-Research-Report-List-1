<p><p>Marine fuel oil can be broadly classified into two types: distillate fuel oil and residual fuel oil. Distillate fuel oil, also known as marine gasoil, is a lighter and cleaner type of fuel that is obtained through the refinement process. It has a lower viscosity and is generally used in smaller ships and vessels due to its lower sulfur content. On the other hand, residual fuel oil, also referred to as heavy fuel oil, is a denser and more impure fuel that remains after the distillate fuel oil has been extracted. Residual fuel oil often contains higher levels of sulfur and is commonly used in larger ships as it is cheaper.</p></p>
