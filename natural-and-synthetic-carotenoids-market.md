<p><p>Carotenoids are naturally occurring pigments found in various plants, algae, and bacteria that contribute to the vibrant colors observed in fruits, vegetables, and other organisms. They play a vital role in human health by acting as antioxidants, protecting against cellular damage, and enhancing immune function. Carotenoids have been linked to a reduced risk of chronic diseases, including cardiovascular diseases, age-related macular degeneration, and certain types of cancer.</p><p>Natural carotenoids are derived from plant and animal sources, such as carrots, tomatoes, paprika, and shrimp, while synthetic carotenoids are chemically produced and have similar chemical structures to their natural counterparts. Both types are used extensively in the food and beverage industry, nutraceuticals, pharmaceuticals, animal feed, and cosmetic products.</p><p>The global market for natural and synthetic carotenoids is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Increasing consumer awareness regarding the health benefits of carotenoids, rising demand for natural colorants in processed food and beverages, and growing healthcare expenditures are key factors driving market growth. Consumers are increasingly seeking products with clean and natural labels, avoiding synthetic additives and coloring agents.</p><p>Overall, the market for natural and synthetic carotenoids is projected to witness a steady growth rate, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12% during the forecast period.
