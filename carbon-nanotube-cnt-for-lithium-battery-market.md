<p><p>Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs) are advanced materials made of carbon atoms arranged in a tubular structure. In the lithium battery market, two types of CNTs are used: Single-Walled Carbon Nanotubes (SWNTs) and Multi-Walled Carbon Nanotubes (MWNTs). SWNTs have a single layer of carbon atoms, while MWNTs have multiple layers concentrically arranged. SWNTs are preferred for their high mechanical strength and electronic properties, making them suitable for use as battery electrodes. MWNTs, on the other hand, offer enhanced electrical conductivity, making them ideal for applications requiring high power and fast charging. Both types contribute to improving the performance and efficiency of lithium batteries.</p></p>
