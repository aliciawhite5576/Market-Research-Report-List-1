<p><p>Isotropic graphite, also known as isomolded graphite, is a type of graphite that possesses uniform properties in all directions. It is made by compression molding fine graphite powder with a resin binder in a special mold and then subjecting it to high-temperature baking and graphitization processes.</p><p>Isotropic graphite is characterized by its excellent thermal conductivity, low electrical resistivity, high mechanical strength, and remarkable chemical resistance. These properties make it highly suitable for various applications such as continuous casting dies, crucibles, heaters, EDM (Electrical Discharge Machining) electrodes, and various other high-temperature and high-performance manufacturing processes.</p><p>The Isotropic Graphite Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 2.30% during the forecast period. <p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Isotropic Graphite Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>CIP Method</li><li>Vibration Molding Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The isotropic graphite market is classified into two types: CIP Method and Vibration Molding Method. In the CIP (Cold Isostatic Pressing) method, graphite powder is compacted in a rubber mold using high pressure. This helps in achieving uniform density and high strength. On the other hand, the Vibration Molding Method involves utilizing a vibrating table to compress graphite mixtures, ensuring better alignment of graphite crystals and enhanced properties. Both methods contribute to the production of high-quality isotropic graphite, each with its distinct advantages.</p></p>
